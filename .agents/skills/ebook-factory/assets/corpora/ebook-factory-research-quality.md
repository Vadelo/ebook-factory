# Ebook Factory Research Quality

## Source Quality

Prefer sources that are specific, current, and accountable. For practical topics, useful sources include official documentation, reputable reports, primary data, expert guides, and well-supported case studies.

Weak sources include anonymous claims, sales pages without evidence, recycled listicles, and posts that promise outcomes without explaining conditions.

## Evidence Levels

Classify claims:

- **Definition**: stable, can be explained from general knowledge.
- **Process**: should be supported by examples or documentation.
- **Current fact**: must be checked against recent sources.
- **High-stakes advice**: requires caveats and stronger sourcing.
- **Income/health/legal claim**: do not present as guaranteed.

## Recency

For topics involving platforms, markets, AI tools, regulation, monetization, or algorithms, assume facts may be stale. Build or refresh the subject-matter corpus with `king-research`, then query it with `kctx`.

## Claim Language

Use calibrated language:

- "can help" instead of "will";
- "often" instead of "always";
- "requires testing" instead of "proven formula";
- "depends on skill, niche, consistency, and market" for business outcomes.

## Research Gap Handling

If the corpus does not support a claim, either remove the claim, mark it as a hypothesis, or re-run research with a more targeted query.

## Corpus Creation Boundary

Use `king-research` to create subject-matter corpora for user ebook themes. Use `kctx` after indexing to search, read, and cite the corpus. Do not treat a generated outline, a prompt, or a manually seeded skill note as a researched topic corpus.
